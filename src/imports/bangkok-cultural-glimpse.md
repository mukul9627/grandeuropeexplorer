Destination Overview: Bangkok
by Grab That Trip 
Bangkok, the vibrant capital of Thailand, offers a perfect introduction to the country’s rich royal heritage and cultural traditions. Carefully curated by Grab That Trip, this itinerary focuses on the city’s most iconic landmarks, sacred temples, and scenic waterways, providing travellers with a well-balanced cultural experience.
The journey highlights Bangkok’s royal past with visits to the Grand Royal Palace and Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha), followed by explorations of revered temples such as Wat Pho, home to the Reclining Buddha, and Wat Traimit, famous for the Golden Buddha. A scenic long-tail boat ride along the Chao Phraya River and historic canals offers a glimpse into traditional riverside life, with a stop at the iconic Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn).
With comfortable hotel stays, smooth airport transfers, guided sightseeing, and relaxed leisure time, this Bangkok experience by Grab That Trip is ideal for travellers seeking a short yet immersive cultural getaway.

Connectivity from India
Bangkok is excellently connected to major cities across India through frequent direct and connecting flights. Several leading airlines operate regular services, making travel convenient and time-efficient for Indian travellers.
Direct flights are available from major Indian metros such as Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai, Kolkata, and Hyderabad to Bangkok’s international airports — Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) and Don Mueang International Airport (DMK). The average flight duration from India to Bangkok ranges between 4 to 5 hours, depending on the departure city.
For travelers from other Indian cities, seamless one-stop connections are available via major hubs. Thailand’s well-developed airport infrastructure ensures smooth immigration procedures and easy onward connectivity within the country.

Travel Requirements for Indian Travellers (Thailand)
To ensure smooth entry into Thailand, Indian travellers should carry:
Valid passport (6+ months validity) 
TDAC digital confirmation: All international travelers, including Indians, must complete the Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) online at least 72 hours before arrival.

Proof of accommodation (hotel booking)

Best Time to Visit
Best season: October to March with cool and pleasant weather, ideal for sightseeing, beaches, and island tours.
Peak season: November to February, perfect for festivals, outdoor activities, and comfortable travel.
Off-season: April to September with lush greenery, fewer crowds, and attractive deals on hotels and packages.

After breakfast, we visit the Grand Royal Palace dedicated to the ceremony of the royal family of Thailand.
