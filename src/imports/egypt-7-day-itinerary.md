Destination Overview: 
Discover the timeless beauty of Egypt with Grab That Trip: Cairo & Nile Cruise Escape, a perfect journey through ancient wonders and scenic landscapes.
Begin in Cairo, exploring the legendary Great Pyramids of Giza, the Sphinx, the National Egyptian Museum, and the historic Citadel of Mohamed Ali. Dive into thousands of years of fascinating history and culture.
Fly to Aswan to board your luxurious 3-night Nile Cruise, sailing along the world’s longest river. Visit the engineering marvel of the High Dam, the beautiful Philae Temple, and the fascinating Unfinished Obelisk.
Cruise onward to discover the remarkable Kom Ombo and Edfu Temples, before arriving in Luxor, known as the world’s greatest open-air museum. Explore the grand Karnak and Luxor Temples, the royal tombs of the Valley of the Kings, the magnificent Temple of Queen Hatshepsut, and the iconic Colossi of Memnon.
This journey blends history, culture, and a relaxing cruise experience, offering an unforgettable Egyptian adventure curated by Grab That Trip.

Best Time to Visit
The best time to visit Egypt is from October to April, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing in Cairo, Luxor, and Aswan.
The summer months (May to September) can be very hot, especially in Upper Egypt, but offer fewer crowds and better pricing.

Day-wise Itinerary
Day 1: ARRIVAL IN CAIRO 
Arrive at the Cairo International Airport, you will be greeted by representative who will meet and assist you at the airport. Then transfer to your hotel. There will be a short briefing with your guide about your itinerary, Check in overnight at your hotel. 
Overnight: Cairo
Day 02: Great Pyramids, Sphinx, National Museums, Citadel of Mohamed Ali
After having breakfast in the hotel, private tour guide will pick up in the morning by a luxury air-conditioned vehicle to pay a visit Giza Necropolis that contains the Great Pyramids of Giza. Stand before the Great Pyramid of Khufu (Cheops), along with the smaller pyramids of Khafre (Chephren) and Menkaure (Micerinus) as well as the Great Sphinx of Giza guarding Khafre Pyramid. The tour includes a visit to the granite temple beside Sphinx known as Temple Valley. Enjoy lunch at a local Egyptian restaurant. After lunch-time, move to visit one of the most important museums in the world, the National Egyptian Museum so as to have an unforgettable tour among the unique pharaonic masterpieces. Move from the museum to Old Cairo so as to the Islamic Saladin Citadel of Cairo and the famous alabaster mosque of Mohamed Ali. Transfer to ur Hotel and overnight in Cairo. Overnight: Cairo
Meals: breakfast, Lunch

Day 3: Nile Cruise Embarkation, Aswan High Dam, Unfinished Obelisk and Temple of Philae Cruise
Check in: Every Mon – Wed – Fri Early Morning check out hotel, transfer to cairo airport flight to Aswan arrive in Aswan, our private Egyptologist guide will drive you to the High Dam of Aswan, one of the significant great industrial achievements. Then you will transfer to your Nile cruise embarkation to check in for 3 nights. Lunch will be served aboard the deluxe Nile cruise. After taking your afternoon tea, meet our private guide again to discover the majestic Philae Temple of Isis. Off to the Unfinished Obelisk which is the largest known ancient one and was intended to complement the so-called Lateran Obelisk. Optional tour take a felucca ride in Aswan - inspiring atmosphere and calm sailing experience to visit Kitchener Island famous for its subtropical, exotic and rare plants and visit Nubian village. Transfer back to your Nile cruise to have an overnight on board. 
Overnight: Nile Cruise 
Meals: breakfast, lunch, and dinner
Day 4: Kom Ombo Temple, Temple of Edfu and Sailing to Luxor
Early Morning 04:00 Am (Optional tour to Visit Abu Simple) from Aswan visit temple of Sobek Afternoon sailing to Luxor, the site of ancient city of Thebes. First visit will be paid to Temple of Kom Ombo located on high grounds overlooking the Nile, which can be dubbed as a double temple built as a dedication to two sets of gods, Haroeris and Sobek during the  Ptolemic Dynasty. From there, sail to Edfu, the site of the Ptolemaic Temple of Horus. Lunch sailing to Edfu, overnight in the Cruise. Overnight: Nile Cruise
Meals: breakfast, lunch, Dinner
Day 5: Edfu temple - Luxor: Luxor Temple and Karnak Temple
Early morning Cruise will arrive in Edfu you will take a tour in Edfu Temple of Horus (also Known as Edfu Temple). Continue sailing to Luxor. Take an unforgettable dinner on board and have an overnight in Luxor afternoon enjoy a day tour in the staggering Karnak Temple Complex, the greatest example of worship in history. Then pay a visit to Luxor Temple, built by Amunhotep III during the New Kingdom. Get back to your Nile cruise to have lunch and enjoy your last night in Luxor. Overnight: Nile Cruise Meals: breakfast, lunch, and dinner.
Day 6: Nile Cruise Disembarkation, Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut, and Colossi of Memnon Back to Cairo by flight 
Early morning 04:30 AM you will have a Optional tours to Do Hot Air Balloon Flight over Luxor West bank and Nile River. A set of stunning views are to be enjoyed from the hot-air balloon flight over the west bank of the Nile River's. Watch Karnak Temple, Hatshepsut Temple, and Valley of Queens from a totally different angle! It is the Egyptian biggest open-air museum on a unique and unforgettable balloon adventure. Make sure that you have your cameras ready to enjoy this unique opportunity of the magnificent panoramic view of Luxor town. Feel the tranquillity of the early morning sun rising over the East Bank, illuminating the city and mountains. Breakfast will be taken on board your Nile cruise after which you will discover the west bank of Luxor by visiting the Valley of the Kings where 18 and 20 Dynasties powerful Pharaohs were buried in the Theban necropolis. Then move beneath Deir el Bahari cliffs to Valley of the Queens where wives of Pharaohs were buried. Visit Temple of Queen Hatshepsut, one of the incomparable monuments of ancient Egypt. On the way back to Luxor east bank, you will visit the Colossi of Memnon, a twin statues of Amenhotep III stand since 1350 BC. Then transfer to Luxor airport to catch the Flight back to Cairo Arrive Cairo airport Transfer to your hotel for last Night take overnight a hotel. 
Overnight: Cairo 
Meals: breakfast, Lunch

Day 7: Final Departure
After check-out, you will be transferred to Cairo international airport for final departure.
Meals: breakfast

FAQs

6 Nights / 7 Days (Cairo & 3-Night Nile Cruise).

3 nights in Cairo (2 at the start, 1 at the end).

3 nights aboard a deluxe Nile Cruise (Aswan to Luxor).

Embarkation is available every Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.

Yes, Cairo–Aswan and Luxor–Cairo flights are included.
6. What meals are included?
Daily breakfast, selected lunches in Cairo, and full board during the Nile Cruise.
7. Are tours private?
Yes, all sightseeing is with a private Egyptologist guide and air-conditioned vehicle.
8. What are the main attractions covered?
Pyramids of Giza, Sphinx, Egyptian Museum, Citadel, Philae Temple, Kom Ombo, Edfu, Karnak, Luxor Temple, Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut Temple, and Colossi of Memnon.
9. Are optional tours available?
Yes – Abu Simbel, Felucca ride & Nubian Village, and Hot Air Balloon in Luxor.
10. What category of accommodation is provided?
4-star hotel in Cairo and a deluxe Nile Cruise.
11. Is tipping included?
No, tipping is optional but customary in Egypt.
12. Is the tour suitable for families and seniors?
Yes, but some sites involve walking and early morning departures.
13. What is the best time to visit?
October to April offers the most pleasant weather.
14. Do I need a visa?
Most nationalities require a tourist visa before travel or visa on arrival (subject to nationality).
15. Is airport assistance included?
Yes, meet and assist service is provided on arrival and departure.
